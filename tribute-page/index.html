We use the internet every day, visiting websites and clicking links in browsers. But the internet was once simply a global information network, until 1991, when Tim Berners-Lee proposed the idea of ​​connecting all data into the World Wide Web (yes, the one known as the WWW). He also created the world's first website, browser, and server, and invented HTML.

Early Life

Tim Berners-Lee was born on June 8, 1955, in London, England, the son of mathematicians and computer scientists Mary Lee Berners-Lee (née Woods; 1924–2017) and Conway Berners-Lee (1921–2019). His parents were both from Birmingham and worked on the Ferranti Mark 1, the first commercially built computer. He has three younger siblings; his brother, Mike, is a professor of ecology and climate change management.

In 1976, Berners-Lee took a first in physics from The Queen's College, Oxford.While there, he made a computer out of an old television set he had purchased from a repair shop.

Career

In 1980, while working at CERN, Berners-Lee proposed a project based on the concept of hypertext to facilitate sharing and updating information among researchers. This project eventually led to the creation of the World Wide Web. He also developed the first web browser and web server software.

In 1994, Berners-Lee founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), which oversees the development of web standards. He has received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to technology and society.

Awards and Honors

  • 1995: Elected a Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS)
  • 2004: Knighted by Queen Elizabeth II for his pioneering work
  • 2007: Named one of the 100 most influential people in the world by Time magazine
  • 2012: Awarded the Turing Award, often referred to as the "Nobel Prize of Computing"
  • 2016: Received the Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering
